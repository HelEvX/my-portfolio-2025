My pro skill set
Front-End Dev

I build static websites with HTML, (S)CSS and 'vanilla' Javascript. My go-to tech stacks also include node.js for build and helper scripts. This portfolio website is perhaps the example of the type of front-end work I do. I'm currently learning React and Vue through online classes, and by working on a web project for a student start-up. I can also work with Bootstrap Grid. I use GitHub for version control and to deploy my projects on Netlify, but I'm still looking to gain more experience in collaborating with developers.

UI, IA & IxD

I learned design principles by being part of several group assignments in college. I've collaborated on building user interfaces for multi-page websites, SaaS platforms and apps. I'm proficient at Figma, still following their monthly online workshops and making sure I stay up to date with product news and release notes. Some designs I've (co-)created are too complex for me to be able to put into code, but on the other hand I've been able to leverage my motion graphics skills to create elaborate mockups of interactive design ideas. I've recently started learning tools to be able to build micro user interactions in code.

UX Research

User experience research has been part of all UI design projects, but I prefer a more thorough approach than what we usually get time for in college group assignments. I've been working on a personal passion project since 2022, for which I completed nearly 30 interviews by videocall, but other commitments have put this huge endeavour on hold for now. I strongly feel that I can count on my academic background for every phase of the UX process and I've noticed my report writing skills are appreciated by tutors and clients alike.

Digital Branding & Marketing

I'm not exactly a sales professional but I do love researching audiences in terms of their wants and needs in relation to a brand's offer. I feel that creating an online presence and personality is the perfect blend of research and design. I use the traditional marketing funnel to plan campaigns, but I'm also very interested in new developments and concerns since the rise of Artificial Intelligence, such as Generative Engine Optimization. I've only created one A-to-Z multi-platform marketing strategy so far for a small-scale entrepreneur as part of a college assignment, but I've had great feedback on it and I'm eager to learn more about it.

Graphic Design

I'm not an illustrator or graphic artist, by which I mean that I don't design characters or draw cartoons, but I'm well-versed in preparing assets for animation as part of my motion graphic design process. I create or use existing vector-based as well as rasterized artwork in my video projects. I also occasionally create poster art, mostly collages and composits with a mix of type art, photography, brush work and vectors. I design logos too of course, and sometimes custom iconography sets, as part of branding projects. My favourite is probably type design and manipulation, especially if I get to play with text animation later.

Motion Design

This is where my journey as a digital designer started and it's still my favourite. I've put all sorts of images and artwork in motion, as a standalone piece or as an asset for a video project. I create branded title and 'thirds' animations, product showreels, bumpers and interstitials in any style. My primary tool is Adobe After Effects and I mostly stick to 2-2.5D visuals (I'm very fond of using parallax effects combined with faux stop-motion), but I've ventured into 3D as well in Blender.

Videography/Photography

I'm mostly familiar with videography from a solopreneur perspective, even though I've done some assisting on student shorts and some camera work for live captation in the past. I have shot and edited full-length events in a multicamera setup as a volunteer with borrowed gear, and ventured into event photography as well using my own camera, but I don't carry the right equipment to shoot professional footage on my own these days. I'm a well-versed and fast editor though, happy to turn a backlog of footage from your business's hard drive into short and snappy reels for social media, websites or a conference room projection screen.
